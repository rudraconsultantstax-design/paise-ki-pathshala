Complete Guide to ITR Filing for Salaried Employees FY 2025-26

Everything you need to know about filing your income tax return — forms, documents, deductions, deadlines and step-by-step process.

April 8, 2026 8 min read Paise Ki Pathshala Team

What is ITR Filing?

Income Tax Return (ITR) is a form that taxpayers use to report their income, deductions, and tax payments to the Income Tax Department. Every salaried individual, freelancer, or business owner whose income exceeds the basic exemption limit must file an ITR every financial year.

For FY 2025-26 (Assessment Year 2026-27), the last date for filing ITR for salaried individuals is typically July 31, 2026. Filing early helps avoid penalties and ensures faster refund processing.

Who Needs to File ITR?

  • Individuals with gross total income exceeding Rs 3,00,000 (under new tax regime)
  • Individuals with gross total income exceeding Rs 2,50,000 (under old tax regime)
  • Persons who want to claim a tax refund
  • Residents with foreign assets or income from outside India
  • Persons who have deposited more than Rs 1 crore in current accounts
  • Persons whose TDS/TCS exceeds Rs 25,000 during the year
  • Persons with electricity expenditure exceeding Rs 1 lakh per year

Documents Required for ITR Filing

  1. PAN Card — Mandatory for all taxpayers
  2. Aadhaar Card — Required for e-verification and linking
  3. Form 16 — TDS certificate from your employer
  4. Form 26AS / AIS — Annual tax statement showing all TDS credits
  5. Bank Statements — For interest income from savings and FDs
  6. Investment Proofs — 80C (PPF, ELSS, LIC), 80D (Health Insurance), 80E (Education Loan)
  7. Rent Receipts — If claiming HRA exemption
  8. Capital Gains Statement — From your broker if you traded stocks or mutual funds
  9. Home Loan Certificate — For deduction under Section 24 and 80EEA

The new tax regime is the default regime from FY 2023-24 onwards. The standard deduction of Rs 75,000 is available under both regimes. However, most deductions under 80C, 80D, HRA etc. are not available under the new regime.

Step-by-Step ITR Filing Process

  1. Step 1: Gather all documents (Form 16, 26AS, AIS, investment proofs)
  2. Step 2: Log in to the Income Tax e-Filing portal (incometax.gov.in)
  3. Step 3: Select the appropriate ITR form (ITR-1 for most salaried individuals)
  4. Step 4: Fill in personal details, income details, deductions, and tax paid
  5. Step 5: Verify all pre-filled data from Form 26AS and AIS
  6. Step 6: Calculate tax liability and pay any remaining tax due
  7. Step 7: Submit the ITR and e-verify using Aadhaar OTP, net banking, or DSC
  8. Step 8: Download the ITR acknowledgment (ITR-V) for your records

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not reporting all sources of income (bank interest, capital gains, freelance income)
  • Choosing the wrong ITR form
  • Not verifying Form 26AS/AIS before filing
  • Missing the filing deadline (penalty of Rs 5,000 under Section 234F)
  • Not claiming all eligible deductions
  • Forgetting to e-verify the return within 30 days of filing

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I file ITR if I have no taxable income?

Yes. Even if your income is below the taxable limit, filing ITR is beneficial for maintaining financial records, claiming refunds, and applying for loans or visas.

What happens if I miss the July 31 deadline?

You can still file a belated return before December 31, but you may face a penalty of up to Rs 5,000 under Section 234F, and interest on unpaid tax under Section 234A.

Is the new tax regime better for salaried employees?

It depends on your deduction profile. If your total deductions under 80C, 80D, HRA, and other sections exceed Rs 3-4 lakhs, the old regime may be more beneficial. Use our Income Tax Calculator to compare both regimes.
