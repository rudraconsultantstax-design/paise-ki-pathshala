GST Registration: Complete Process, Documents & Eligibility in India

Everything you need to register under GST — eligibility criteria, required documents, online process, types of registration, and key compliance obligations.

What is GST Registration?

GST (Goods and Services Tax) registration is the process by which a taxpayer gets enrolled under India's indirect tax system. Once registered, a business receives a unique 15-digit GSTIN (Goods and Services Tax Identification Number). This number must be used on all invoices and tax filings.

GST registration is mandatory for businesses crossing the prescribed turnover threshold, and voluntary for smaller businesses who want to avail input tax credits or appear credible to larger customers.

Documents Required for GST Registration

For Proprietorship

  • PAN card of the proprietor
  • Aadhaar card of the proprietor
  • Photograph of the proprietor
  • Bank account details (cancelled cheque / bank statement)
  • Business address proof (electricity bill / rent agreement)

For Private Limited Company / LLP

  • PAN card of the company
  • Certificate of Incorporation
  • Memorandum of Association (MoA) and Articles of Association (AoA)
  • PAN and Aadhaar of all directors
  • Board resolution for authorized signatory
  • Bank account details
  • Registered office address proof

Step-by-Step GST Registration Process

  1. Step 1: Visit GST Portal (gst.gov.in) and click on "New Registration"
  2. Step 2: Fill Part-A with PAN, mobile number, and email. Verify with OTP.
  3. Step 3: Receive Temporary Reference Number (TRN) on email and mobile
  4. Step 4: Log in using TRN and fill Part-B with business details, promoter info, and documents
  5. Step 5: Upload all required documents in prescribed format (PDF/JPEG)
  6. Step 6: Submit application using DSC / EVC / Aadhaar OTP
  7. Step 7: Receive Application Reference Number (ARN) via email/SMS
  8. Step 8: GST officer reviews and approves within 7 working days. GSTIN is issued.

Key GST Returns to File After Registration

  • GSTR-1: Monthly/quarterly return for outward supplies (sales)
  • GSTR-3B: Monthly summary return with tax payment
  • GSTR-9: Annual return (mandatory for turnover above Rs 2 crore)
  • GSTR-2B: Auto-drafted input tax credit statement

Penalties for Non-Registration

If a business fails to register under GST despite being required to do so, the penalty is 10% of the tax due (minimum Rs 10,000). In cases of deliberate non-registration, the penalty can be 100% of the tax amount.
